The History of Miso

Miso is a Japanese fermented seasoning, and essential for Japanese food tradition.

Miso soup is the most common cuisine and has many types. By the 10th century, there is a record of miso as a fermented seasoning using salt, rice and soy.
Miso soup became popular in 14th century, and had been a mainstream of Japanese seasoning until soy source become popular.
